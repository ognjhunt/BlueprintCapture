# Capturer Marketing And Copy Positioning

Date: 2026-05-13

Status: repo-grounded messaging system. This is not a compensation policy, launch approval, provider-readiness decision, or smart-glasses launch declaration.

## Doctrine Baseline

BlueprintCapture is the evidence-capture client. It records real-site walkthrough evidence, preserves raw sensor data, packages a canonical raw bundle, and uploads that bundle for downstream bridge and Pipeline processing.

The copy system must preserve these boundaries:

- Capture is the entry point. Qualification comes after truthful evidence capture.
- Raw capture, timestamps, poses, intrinsics, depth, device metadata, rights, privacy, and provenance remain authoritative.
- Generated scenes, world-model outputs, previews, and review artifacts are downstream products. They are not captured truth.
- In-app hints can coach capture quality and task flow. They must not decide payout, rights, buyer trust, provider readiness, or commercialization.
- Payout eligibility must come from real upstream assignment, backend review, rights scope, quality review, and provider configuration. It must not be inferred from place type, estimated effort, or demo data.
- Smart glasses are optional capture devices for supported/internal paths. They must not be messaged as a public launch-ready surface without physical-device proof and downstream Pipeline/WebApp proof for the same capture chain.

## Recommended Positioning

Core public positioning:

> Capture real places for robot teams. Use your phone to record guided walkthrough evidence, upload the raw bundle, and track review.

Expanded public positioning:

> BlueprintCapture helps approved capturers record real places so Blueprint can build rights-aware, provenance-safe site-specific world-model packages for robot teams. Captures are reviewed for quality, scope, rights, and downstream eligibility before any payout or buyer-facing use.

Pilot positioning:

> Join the first Blueprint capturer cohort. We are opening limited phone-based capture assignments city by city. Eligible assignments show task details and any quoted payout before capture; every upload still goes through quality, rights, and scope review.

Internal positioning:

> BlueprintCapture is the supply-side evidence layer for the capture -> package -> buyer flywheel. It should make capture tasks easy to understand and complete without turning review, payout, provider, or rights status into in-app promises.

## Approved And Blocked Claims

Approved public-safe claims:

- "Capture with your phone."
- "Blueprint reviews quality, rights, scope, and downstream eligibility after upload."
- "Approved assignments may show a quoted payout before capture."
- "Open captures are review-gated submissions."
- "The app preserves raw video, motion, timestamps, device metadata, and available pose/depth signals."
- "Generated scenes and downstream packages are derived from captured evidence."
- "Smart glasses are optional for supported capture paths."
- "Availability is limited by city, assignment supply, device support, and review state."

Claims requiring a qualifier:

- "Get paid to capture real places" -> only use as "Eligible pilot assignments may include quoted payouts after review."
- "Available near you" -> only use when backed by live city and target supply.
- "Approved capture" -> only use for real approved jobs or org-backed launch targets.
- "Wallet" -> use as "wallet status/history" unless live payout provider proof exists.
- "Referral rewards" -> use only when the referral backend and payout provider are ready; otherwise "invite review-gated capturers."

Blocked claims:

- Average, guaranteed, or minimum earnings without an approved compensation policy.
- "$40 average" as public copy without founder-approved policy and backend support.
- "Cash out anytime" unless the payout provider and instant-payout path are live for that cohort.
- "Every capture earns" or "any place can be paid."
- "Rights-cleared," "buyer-ready," "launch-ready," or "provider-ready" from capture completion alone.
- "Smart glasses launch-ready" without physical-device proof and downstream proof.
- "Generated world model is capture truth."

## Payout Language Tiers

Public-safe default:

- "Some approved assignments may include a quoted payout."
- "Payout eligibility is reviewed after upload."
- "Blueprint checks capture quality, rights, scope, and account/payout setup before payout."
- "Open captures are review-gated and may not include a quoted payout."
- "If payouts are not enabled for this build or cohort, the app should say so plainly."

Pilot-only:

- "This assignment has a quoted payout of $X. Final eligibility depends on quality, rights, scope, and account verification."
- "Your upload is under review. We will show approved, needs-fix, rejected, and paid states separately."
- "Payout setup is available for this pilot cohort after identity and provider verification."
- "Standard payouts are available after approved captures and provider enablement."

Internal hypothesis:

- "$40 average per eligible capture" can be considered only as a draft compensation hypothesis. It needs founder approval, backend policy, legal/tax review, and product-state support before appearing in public copy.
- The existing internal iOS pricing heuristic uses a separate $50/hour estimate for ranking and nearby target math. Treat it as non-public until it is replaced by backend quotes or an approved compensation policy.
- Do not convert internal estimates into "quoted" UI copy.

## Smart-Glasses Language Rules

Allowed:

- "Optional for supported hands-free capture."
- "Phone capture is available first; glasses can be connected later when supported."
- "Meta glasses and Android XR paths are internal or limited until device proof and downstream proof exist."
- "Target-scoped glasses capture finalizes into the same raw bundle/upload pipeline when enabled."

Blocked:

- "Smart-glasses launch is ready."
- "Capture with glasses anywhere."
- "Connect glasses to unlock paid jobs."
- "Android XR is public-ready."
- Any copy that implies Meta device support is available for all capturers, all cities, or all assignments.

## First-25 Capturer Cohort

Recommended name: "Founding Capturer Cohort."

Positioning:

> We are inviting the first 25 careful capturers to help Blueprint test phone-based real-place capture. Cohort members learn the capture flow, submit review-gated walkthroughs, and may receive eligible quoted assignments as city supply opens. We will be explicit about which captures are review-only, which are approved assignments, and which payouts require additional review.

What to emphasize:

- Phone-first capture.
- Clean walkthrough technique.
- Access and privacy judgment.
- Review feedback and recapture loops.

- Guaranteed assignment volume.

- Active supply in every city.

## Existing Copy Audit

Current findings from 2026-05-13:

- README framing used "three-sided marketplace." This can over-center marketplace browsing, so it should be reframed as a capture-to-world-model platform with capturer, site-operator, and robot-team lanes.
- iOS nearby target rows displayed a heuristic dollar value as "Quoted." That was too strong because the value came from local estimation, not a backend assignment quote. It should be "Review gated" unless a real `quoted_payout_cents` value is present.
- Android auth said "Sign up to track earnings and get paid." That is too broad for alpha/provider-gated state. It should say "track capture reviews and eligible payouts."
- Smart-glasses onboarding should describe glasses as optional and supported/limited, never as launch-ready.
- A Swift comment referenced "Kled AI style." Remove competitor-branded implementation comments.
- `BlueprintCapture/Models/SkuPricing.swift` contains a local $50/hour payout heuristic. It is not a public compensation policy. Keep it internal, avoid public copy based on it, and replace with backend quotes before public launch.
- Root-level Stripe/setup summaries still contain stale implementation-era wording such as mock earnings and production-ready claims. They are historical docs, not public copy, but should be excluded from public messaging and cleaned separately if this repo is being packaged for external review.
- Internal docs still use "marketplace" for demand ranking and operational mechanics. That is acceptable internally when it describes supply/demand logic, but public capturer copy should lead with capture, review, and site-specific world-model value.

## Founder-Approval Blockers

These must be approved before public use:

- Any public average payout claim, including "$40 average."
- Any fixed minimum, guaranteed, or instant payout claim.
- Any city-specific availability claim.
- Any statement that a cohort has live paid assignments.
- Any smart-glasses public launch claim.
- Any referral reward claim.
- Any claim that rights are cleared, buyer use is authorized, or downstream commercialization is approved.
